Point of View. Owners and senior staff of mid-sized German Hausverwaltungen need a way to dramatically reduce the time they
spend on routine administrative work — tenant emails, owner-assembly minutes, compliance tracking and craftsman
coordination — because they currently lose roughly 36 of every 60 working hours to these tasks, which blocks them from
handling rising unit counts, shrinking staff and tightening WEG/DSGVO obligations. 
1. TOP 3 “HOW MIGHT WE” QUESTIONS
1
2

How might we help mid-sized Hausverwaltungen handle routine tenant communication automatically so that property
managers can spend their working time on owner relationships and decisions that legally require a human.
How might we help small property-management teams stay continuously compliant with WEG, DSGVO and
Betriebskosten deadlines so that legal risk shrinks without hiring a dedicated compliance officer.
How might we help Hausverwaltungen coordinate maintenance and craftsmen with minimal back-and-forth so that tenants
get faster fixes and managers stop drowning in phone calls and email chains.

Mexico City — Module 3: Problem–Solution Fit
3. CHOSEN IDEA
ONE-SENTENCE IDEA
Page 2 / 2
An AI communication co-pilot for German Hausverwaltungen that drafts, classifies and
prioritises tenant emails — so Sabine reviews and approves instead of writing from
scratch.
CORE FEATURE (MVP)
• 
• 
• 
• 
• 
Inbox triage: incoming tenant mails are classified (urgent /
routine / informational) and ranked.
Auto-drafted replies: Valta proposes a reply grounded in
the property file, lease terms and prior thread. Sabine
reviews, edits, sends.
Multilingual: understands and replies in DE / EN / TR /
RU / AR — Sabine works only in German.
Active reminders: overdue rent, expiring contracts,
missing documents — sent automatically with the firm’s
tone.
Sits on top of existing ERP (Domus / Haufe / Impower) —
no migration required.
WHY WE SELECTED THIS IDEA
PROBLEM–SOLUTION FIT
Email and routine writing is the
single largest slice of Sabine’s
week (~30% of her 60h). The
MVP directly cuts it, not a
peripheral pain.
